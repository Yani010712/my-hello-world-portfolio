About Me

Yani Rodriguez

I was born and raised in Florida, not in the state of Florida, but a small town in Cuba with the same name. I do not remember having passion for any profession when I was younger so when I finished high school I entered the University of Computer Science of Havana only for the wish to go to university, but in reality, I had never had a computer in my life, so my knowledge of computing was vary basic at that time.

I graduated without obstacles, but without much enthusiasm. Two years later, I was lucky enough to emigrate to the United States, where for reasons that no one knows I started working as a TV producer. In the beginning, it was enjoyable, but after a while, I began to miss working in technology and resigned that job.

Was then when I realized that it had been five years and the world of technology moved very fast, and I had stayed behind, it was then when I began to research how I could get back on track with technology and discovered Wyncode, a coding Bootcamp. For the first time in my life, I feel that I found my true passion, and I am pleased about that.

Now that I love being a software engineer, I am very interested in using innovative technology to solve educational, social, and environmental problems.

My coding interests include web development and software testing, including manual and automation. I also have an ISTQB (International Software Testing Qualification Board) certification. I am a team player with a can-do attitude. I am very capable of working under pressure and very motivated to learn.

Trained in programming languages (OOP, JavaScript, HTML, CSS), web frameworks (MVC, Bootstrap, Jquery, React), database management (MySQL, PostgreSQL), and project management and product development (Github, Agile/Scrum)

As a tv producer, I got experience writing scripts, as well as working with Avid products like Inews, Interplay|MAM, Media|Central, Media Composer.

I love coffee, to build origami, and play Sudoku.
